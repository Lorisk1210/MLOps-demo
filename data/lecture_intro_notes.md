# Session 1 Notes - Introduction and Course Structure

Source: `../slides/01-MLOps-lecture-Introduction.pdf`

## Course Structure
- The lecture sequence starts with "From Models to Systems" and "Trustworthy AI & Regulation".
- Session 2 covers the ML cycle plus fine-tuning and transfer learning.
- Sessions 3 and 4 are seminar topic presentations.
- Sessions 5 and 6 are demo presentations.

## Assessment Expectations
- Students should combine theory and practice rather than presenting only conceptual material.
- The seminar-topic presentation is worth 40% and includes a Q&A portion.
- The demo presentation is worth 40% and should showcase at least one tool with prepared examples in a meaningful context.
- Peer review is worth 20% and requires constructive, topic-focused feedback on assigned peer presentations.

## Teaching Context
- Prof. Clara Winters has a software systems and process engineering background and serves as professor at Northlake University.
- Prof. Leon Richter teaches AI and machine learning topics in the course.
