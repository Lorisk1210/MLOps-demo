# Trustworthy AI and Regulation Notes

Source: `../slides/03-lecture-Trustworthy_AI_Regulation.pdf`

## Why Regulation Matters
- The lecture frames modern AI development as a tension between innovation and regulation rather than a simple trade-off.
- Trustworthy AI discussions focus on risk management, governance, and real-world impact.

## Key Risk Areas
- Important ML and AI risks include bias and fairness, safety, security, accountability, quality standardization, explainability, transparency, ethics, intellectual property protection, and deepfakes.
- Regulation and standards are presented as responses to these risks, especially when AI systems affect people at scale.

## Relevant Governance Themes
- GDPR Article 22 is highlighted as relevant for automated decision-making and profiling.
- Trustworthy AI requires human oversight, robustness and safety, privacy and data quality management, transparency, fairness, accessibility, and positive social impact.
- Standardization efforts from organizations such as IEEE and ISO are discussed as part of the broader governance landscape.
